Topic modeling - Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)

LDA is a statistical model often used in natural language processing (NLP). It maps a document into a distribution (Dirichlet) of hidden (latent) "topics", which itself is a distribution of words. So you can think of these topics as groupings of associated words. These topics are generated by statistical distribution of words within each document, inside of a corpus of documents.

The biggest advantage of LDA is that it makes the data very interpretable. Topics are simply distribution of words. Therefore, you can get an idea of what each topic by looking at the words with highest weights! This is crucial in many fields, where a black box model cannot be used.
